But if you calculate E21−1E_{21}^{-1}E21−1​ and E32−1E_{32}^{-1}E32−1​, you will find that E−1=E21−1E32−1=[100210051]E^{-1}=E_{21}^{-1}E_{32}^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 5 & 1\end{bmatrix}E−1=E21−1​E32−1​=​120​015​001​​. So we can get E−1E^{-1}E−1 directly.

Permutation #

You can use permutation to exchange rows. For example, P21=[010100001]P_{21}=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}P21​=​010​100​001​​ means exchange row 1 and row 2. And P32=[100001010]P_{32}=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0\end{bmatrix}P32​=​100​001​010​​ means exchange row 2 and row 3.

  1. P−1=PTP^{-1}=P^TP−1=PT.
  2. In matrix elimination, if pivot is zero, we should exchange rows. Now A=LUA=LUA=LU becomes PA=LUPA=LUPA=LU. It just exchanges A’s rows to let pivot in each step be nonzero.
